Ham and Cheese Sliders

Description

Are you looking for a way to elevate your ham and cheese sandwich experience? Are you hosting a party and need a dish that will be a hit? Do you love King's Hawaiian rolls?

If you said yes to any of these questions, I've got just the recipe for you! This one is a fan favorite and a staple at any game day party that I'm attending. All you really need is ham, cheese, rolls, and butter, but that extra drizzle on top really does add that little bit of oomph that everyone loves in a good slider.

Ingredients

This recipe doesn't call for specific quantities of each ingredient, so those decisions are left to the chef (and hey, that's you!)

  • King's Hawaiian rolls
  • Ham (honey, smoked, deli, pre-packaged - it all works the same!)
  • Your favorite cheese, but swiss is a classic pairing
  • Mayonnaise (or Miracle Whip, if that's more your cup of tea)
  • Melted butter
  • Dijon mustard
  • Worcestershire sauce
  • Onion powder
  • Poppy seeds

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 400Â°.
  2. Slice the rolls in half horizontally. Do your best to keep the rolls connected to each other, but hey, accidents happen!
  3. Set the top section aside and add the bottom section to a baking dish (any size dish, as long as the rolls fit inside!)
  4. Spread a layer of mayo on the insides of both sections.
  5. Layer the ham and cheese evenly across the bottom section of rolls, then place the top section of rolls back on top.
  6. Whisk together the butter, dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, onion powder, and poppy seeds in a small bowl. Feel free to taste as you go, customizing the sauce to your liking!
  7. Brush the butter mixture evenly over the top of the fully constructed sliders.
  8. Let the sliders sit for 5-10 minutes to fully soak in the flavor that makes them so darn delicious.
  9. Cover the dish with foil and bake until the cheese is melted and the top is golden-brown.
  10. Optional: eat the whole pan in one sitting!
